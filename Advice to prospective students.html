“What advice would we give a prospective student”?

1) Take advantage of the seminars/webinars periods offered by the university.

2) One of the perks of the African Leadership University is the diverse culture of the university – get to know students from all over the African continent. Reach out to other students outide your classes. Forming relationships encourages diversity and togetherness in the college community

3) Reach out to your facilitators if you have questions. They work on a mostly volunteer basis and are happy to explain concepts or point out additional resources. Some have even offered to upload helpful videos on YouTube if certain topics are not clear enough. The university is lucky to have so many instructors who are passionate about the courses and modules they teach.

4) Time management is vital! Ensure to get your work done on time but meeting up the deadline so you do not become stressed and anxious then lose marks about it later on. It also gives you more time to talk with your module facilitator if you are having troubles completing them.

5) Make effective use of content material and resources provided by module leaders.

6) Take ownership of your experiences at the university.

7) Seek out valued opportunities.

8) Do not get your expectations too high in order to avoid dissapointment and demotivation.

ABOUT ALU

ALU aims to develop 3 million ethical and entrepreneurial leaders for Africa and the world by 2035. It uses a personalized, student-driven, project-based, and mission-oriented approach to create agile, lifelong learners who can adapt to a changing world. ALU's first site was inaugurated in September 2015 in Mauritius. Its second campus was inaugurated in September 2017 in Kigali Heights, Rwanda.
